COOKIES?

Not the edible, the computer kind!

Sorry, I knew you were excited about seeing cookies, but I promise this is worth the read! Cookies are text files that websites send to your browser; this enables you to have personalized experiences. Cookies websites to make things easier to use and more personalized to each person. It even allows companies to store your shopping carts or remember what websites you have visited. Let’s just say you go to buy a baseball bat, you type in “Kids baseball bat” and now you start getting personalized ads on amazon from baseball company’s those are called Third-party cookies. That’s something we will learn more about later. While cookies can make things personalized and generally tend to be safe, there are a few risks associated with cookies. Hackers can easily trick users into accidentally giving up their information with simple tactics. Recently there has been a new scam that sends a text to millions of people asking them to pay for tolls in states that they have never been in. Hackers are asking users to click on a website to pay the tolls. When the user clicks on the toll link they are able to access all of the users information through the site, which is referred to as Cross-site Scripting. While there are many pros and cons of cookies, each user should weigh the risk of allowing cookies to access their browsing history.
